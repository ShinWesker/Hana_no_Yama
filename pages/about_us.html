about us

Hana no Yama's genesis traces back to the unyielding passion for traditional Japanese cuisine held by our founders, culinary maestros Kenji Hayashi and Minato Tanaka. Both were born into families steeped in the world of fine dining; Hiroshi, a third-generation sushi chef, and Keiko, whose parents owned a prestigious kaiseki ryori restaurant in Kyoto. In 2017, inspired by their shared reverence for their country's culinary heritage, they embarked on a quest to create a dining experience that would not only mirror the exquisite artistry of traditional Japanese cuisine but also introduce a novel dimension to it. Hana no Yama, which translates to 'Mountain of Flowers', emerged as a poetic representation of their vision—an establishment that harmoniously fused tradition with innovation, just as a mountain in bloom embodies both the eternal and the ephemeral. The restaurant was meticulously designed to emanate a sense of tranquility and elegance, with the ambiance itself serving as an homage to the beauty of Japan's natural landscape. The menu, meanwhile, reflected a deep respect for seasonal ingredients and the art of kaiseki, while innovative elements infused a refreshing contemporary twist. The founders' commitment to exceptional quality and service quickly earned Hana no Yama a distinguished reputation among discerning diners. Today, the restaurant stands as a beacon of Japanese fine dining, a testament to the founders' unrelenting pursuit of culinary excellence, and an embodiment of their belief in sharing the rich tapestry of Japanese culinary tradition with the world.

Kenji Hayashi & Minato Tanaka

Kenji Hayashi's voyage into the realm of gastronomy began in the bustling food markets of Osaka, where his family owned a prosperous fishmonger business. Immersed in an environment brimming with the freshest sea produce, Kenji developed an early appreciation for quality ingredients. As he observed the chefs in nearby restaurants selecting their daily catch with careful precision, he became intrigued by the transformative process that turned humble ingredients into culinary masterpieces. Guided by an insatiable curiosity and an inherent respect for his country's culinary traditions, Kenji enrolled in Tokyo's prestigious culinary school, cultivating his skills under the guidance of celebrated sushi masters. Today, he employs his finely honed skills and intimate knowledge of seafood to bring an unparalleled sushi experience to the patrons of Hana no Yama.

Minato Tanaka was born and raised in the ancient city of Kyoto, known for its refined culinary culture. Growing up as the son of a tea ceremony master, Minato's life was steeped in tradition and the art of hospitality. His interest in cooking sparked when he realized how beautifully food and hospitality intertwined in kaiseki ryori, the traditional multi-course dining that had its origins in tea ceremony. Seeking to further this initial interest, Minato apprenticed at a renowned kaiseki ryori restaurant in Kyoto, absorbing the intricate details of this complex cuisine. His dedication and relentless pursuit of mastery earned him the position of head chef at a young age. Today, at Hana no Yama, Minato combines his deep understanding of the kaiseki tradition with a modern approach, creating dishes that honor the past while engaging the present.
